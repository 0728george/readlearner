# Solving Quadratic Equations: A Complete Guide

The quadratic equation is one of the most fundamental concepts in algebra:

$$
ax^2 + bx + c = 0
$$

## The Quadratic Formula

$$
x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
$$

## Step-by-Step Example

Letâ€™s solve:  
$2x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0$

1. $a = 2$, $b = -4$, $c = -6$
2. Discriminant: $\Delta = (-4)^2 - 4(2)(-6) = 16 + 48 = 64$
3. $x = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{64}}{4} = \frac{4 \pm 8}{4}$
4. $x_1 = 3$, $x_2 = -1$
